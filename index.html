I am a first year CS PhD student in the UNC NLP lab at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill, advised by Prof. Mohit Bansal. Previously, I spent 1.5 years as Data Scientist at Sciome LLC, a budding health-informatics startup based out of Durham. I built Information Extraction pipelines for parsing scientific articles and converting the full-text into meaningful, structured data. I also built better models for gene expression extrapolation and toxicological assay image analysis.

I graduated with a Masters in Biomedical & Health Informatics from University of Washington School of Medicine and worked towards a thesis on conversion of clinical discharge summaries to patient timeline with Prof. Meliha Yetisgen. At the same time, I was also a Research Assistant advised by Prof. Elaine O. Nsoesie at the Institute of Health Metrics & Evaluation.

I completed my undergraduate (Integrated B.Tech & M.Tech) in Electronics & Electrical Communication Engineering, with specialization in Visual Info. Processing & Embedded Systems from Indian Institute of Technology, Kharagpur, India in 2015. I had research stints as an intern at the National Remote Sensing Center, Hyderabad, India and German Cancer Research Center, Heidleberg, Germany. My broad research interests are in Natural Language Processing, Computational Linguistics, Machine Learning and Deep Learning.
